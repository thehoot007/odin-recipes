Lasagne al Forno

Home

Description

A classic Italian recipe made entirely from scratch.

Ingredients

Ragu

  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 cup carrot, finely chopped
  • 1 cup celery, finely chopped
  • 1 cup white onion, finely chopped
  • 1 lb ground beef (mince)
  • 1 lb ground pork (mince)
  • 2.5 cups Passata (sieved tomatoes)
  • 3 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 cup red wine
  • 6 cups beef stock
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 tsp sea salt flakes and pepper

White Sauce

  • 5 tbsp butter
  • 5 tbsp all-purpose flour
  • 4 cubs full fat milk
  • ½ tsp nutmeg
  • 1 cup parmesan, freshly grated
  • 1 tsp sea salt and pepper, to taste

Lasagne

  • 1 lb fresh lasagne sheets
  • 2 balls mozzarella

Steps

Ragu

  1. Finely chop the carrot, onion and celery and saute the vegetables gently in a large frying pan with the olive oil. Once the vegetables are soft add the beef and pork mince and cook until browned.
  2. If there is much excess fat in the pan, spoon some out. Add the red wine and reduce by half.
  3. Once the wine has reduced, add the passata, tomato paste, bay leaves, 4 cups of beef stock and a pinch of salt and pepper.
  4. Stir everything together and leave to simmer on low heat for 2.5-3 hours uncovered. Add the rest of the beef stock half way through.

White Sauce

  1. Add the butter to a saucepan and cook until melted and bubbling.
  2. Add the flour to the melted butter and stir to form a paste. Let the flour cook for 1 minute.
  3. Slowly whisk half of the milk into the butter and flour constantly whisking to avoid any lumps. Once it has started to thicken add the rest of the milk, nutmeg, parmesan and a pinch of salt and pepper.
  4. Continue to heat the sauce whilst stirring until thickened enought to coat the back of a wooden spoon. Take off the heat and set aside.

Assembling the Lasagne

  1. Preheat oven to 350℉ (180℃)
  2. To assemble the lasagne, spoon a small amount of ragu on the very bottom of the baking dish. Top with an even layer of lasagne sheets.
  3. Add a few more spoons of ragu so the pasta is completely covered, followed by 2 ladels of white sauce.
  4. Repeat the layers of pasta, ragu and bechamel sauce until everything is used up making sure to keep enough white sauce for the very top layer (you should have 4-5 layers of pasta).
  5. Cover the top layer of lasagne with torn mozzarella and then bake in the oven for 45 minutes or until bubbling and golden.
  6. Let it cool slightly for 5-10 minutes before serving.
