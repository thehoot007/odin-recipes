Deviled Eggs

Home

Description

Arguably everybody's favourite egg recipe! Easy, affordable party food that's excellent for making ahead.

Ingredients

  • 12 large eggs

Deviled Eggs Filling

  • 2 tsp Dijon mustard
  • 2 tsp Tabasco or other hot sauce
  • 1 tsp white wine vinegar
  • tsp mayonnaise , whole egg mayo preferred, or kewpie
  • 1/4 heaped teaspoon cooking salt / kosher salt
  • 50 g/ 3 tbsp cold unsalted butter , cut into 1 cm / 0.2â€³ cubes

Garnish

  • Paprika, regular, sweet, or smoked
  • 1 tbsp chives, finely chopped

Steps

Hard boil eggs with centred yolks:

  1. Large pot — Bring a large pot with 15cm/6 water to the boil. Lower eggs in gently using a spider or slotted spoon. Lower heat a tiny bit if needed to stop the eggs from jostling around so much they crack.
  2. Boild and swirl — Boil eggs for 10 minutes. Swirl the water every minute for the first 6 minutes, using the handle of a wooden spoon. Give the water 4 or 4 fast stirs in one direction to make the water and eggs swirl in the water. The centrifugal force will make the yolks cook centred in the egg white (i.e. no thin broken egg white walls)
  3. Peel — Fill a sink with cold tap water. Remove eggs from the boiling water using a slotted spoon or spider. Leave for 5 minutes until cool enough to handle. Tap the base then peel underwater.

Fill devilled eggs:

  1. Remove yolks — Cut the eggs in half and remove the yolks using a teaspoon.
  2. Filling — Place the yolks and all filling ingredients except butter in a small food processor. Blits until yolks are broken up. Add butter and blits, scraping down the sides as needed, until the butter is blended through. The filling should be creamy (smear with back of spoon to check).
  3. Choose the best 18 to 20 egg white halves. Brush yolk crumbs off the cut surface.
  4. Pipe — Transfer filling into a piping bag fitted with a nozzle of your choice. Fill so it's mounded.
  5. Sprinkle with paprika and chives, then serve.
